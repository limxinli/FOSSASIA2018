Improved Water Source Availability

Cambodia

In Cambodia, 6.3 million out of 14.9 million Cambodians lack access to clean water. Water facilities and infrastructure is limited outside the main cities. This remains a challenge especially for the rural households where the water supply is made less of a priority.
[Source: OpenDevelopment | Cambodia]

Laos

Water is of critical importance for meeting food, water and energy demands of communities in the region. Forest catchments and watersheds plays a huge responsibility in generating and capturing rain. However, pollution and decresing water levels pose a threat to meeting these needs.
[Source: OpenDevelopment]

Thailand

Thailand possess abundant water resources. There has been a reduced of renewable internal freshwater resources per capita comparing from 1962 to 2014. It is an increase water scarcity contributing to prolonged dry seasons in Thailand.
[Source: OpenDevelopment | Thailand]

Vietnam

In 2016, Vietnam's had reached 92.7 million people, which means that substantial numbers of people still lack access to basic services. In 2015, 8.2 million people still lacked ‘basic’ access to a water supply that does not involve more than a 30 minute return journey to access clean water.
[Source: OpenDevelopment | Vietnam]
